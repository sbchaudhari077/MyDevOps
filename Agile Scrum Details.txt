Agile - Scrum

Scrum Roles
1. Scrum Master: A facilitator or lead support
2. Product owner: Own product/story backlogs, only PO can prioritized item based on importance. And intermediate between team and stakeholder
3. Teams (Development/QA/BA): Generally 3-9 including business analyst architect, developers, testers

Scrum Master/Product Owner Business Pillars
1. Transparency 
2. Inspection
3. Adaption

Scrum Metrix/Artifacts:
1. Burn Down chart
2. Velocity Chart
3. Scrum Kanban board

Scrum Principals
1. Empirical process control:
	Learning from practice or experience or reality.
2. Self Organization:
	Team/organization themself instead manager or lead tell them. Prioritize item or take work based on priority or business need.
3. Collaboration:
4. Value base prioritization:
5. Time Boxing
6. Iterative development, not sequential.

Scrum Values:
1. Commitment
2. Courage: To take decision to achieve goals.
3. Focus: Focus on delivery
4. Openness:  Be open on status difficulties what we are facing
5. Respect: Respect on openness, what we are doing

Scrum Aspects
1. Organization
2. Business Justification: why we are doing
3. Quality: Business acceptance criteria 
4. Change: Become change and add it.
5. Risk: Identify small, medium and high risk and try to reduce and eliminate.

Scrum strength
1. Continuous delivery
2. Empowerment
3. Cross functional teams
4. Self organization
5. Focus on short term objective, iterative approach.
